# Missionaria Protectiva

The [[bene-gesserit]] practice of seeding religious prophecies and myths on primitive worlds as insurance — so a stranded operative can invoke them for protection.

## Definition

The Missionaria Protectiva is a centuries-long Bene Gesserit program to embed religious myths in cultures across the Imperium. When Bene Gesserit missionaries visit a world, they plant specific prophecies into the local religion:
- A messiah will come from off-world
- He will be accompanied by a Bene Gesserit woman (his mother)
- He will lead the people to a better future

The purpose is purely pragmatic: if a Bene Gesserit is ever stranded on that world, she can claim to be the prophecy — or her son can. The locals' own belief system becomes a survival tool.

## Discovery on Arrakis

[[lady-jessica]] recognized the Missionaria Protectiva prophecies embedded in [[the-fremen]] religious culture in Ch3. She realized:
1. The prophecies are genuine Bene Gesserit plant
2. She and [[paul-atreides]] fit the prophecy perfectly:
   - Paul = the off-world messiah
   - Jessica = his Bene Gesserit mother

## Dual Nature: Tool and Danger

Jessica understands this is both:

**Tool:** If the Atreides situation deteriorates, she and Paul could invoke these prophecies to gain Fremen protection and authority. In extremis, a prophet-figure commands enormous power.

**Danger:** Using the Missionaria Protectiva means building everything on a manipulated belief system. The Fremen's faith would be based on a lie. Paul might become something he cannot control — a figure of religious devotion, not just political alliance. And messianic movements are unpredictable.

## Open Questions

- Will Jessica and Paul invoke the prophecies deliberately?
- Does Paul already fit the prophecy in ways beyond Jessica's planting?

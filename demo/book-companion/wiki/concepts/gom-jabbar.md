# Gom Jabbar

The [[bene-gesserit]] test of humanity: subject endures simulated intense pain while a poisoned needle is held to their neck — withdrawal means death.

## Definition

"Gom jabbar" literally means "the high-handed enemy." The test uses two components:
1. A box that induces intense, realistic pain in the hand placed inside it (the pain is simulated — no physical damage occurs)
2. A needle at the subject's neck, coated with meta-cyanide — immediate death if the needle touches

The test lasts several minutes. The subject must keep their hand in the box and not withdraw it.

## Philosophy

The Bene Gesserit believe the defining characteristic of humanity is the ability to override animal instinct with reason. An animal, feeling overwhelming pain, withdraws — even at the cost of its own survival (like an animal chewing off a leg to escape a trap). A human can override this.

> "I hold at your neck a gom jabbar. The gom jabbar — the high-handed enemy. It kills only animals."

To withdraw is to fail — to be, in their view, still primarily an animal. To endure is to be human.

## Paul's Test (Chapter 1)

[[reverend-mother-mohiam]] administered the test to [[paul-atreides]] in Ch1. Paul:
- Controlled his fear through mental discipline from the moment the box appeared
- Endured the pain without withdrawing for several minutes
- Demonstrated Bene Gesserit-level perception by reading Mohiam's own fear during the test

Her assessment: he is human. And possibly much more — see [[kwisatz-haderach]].

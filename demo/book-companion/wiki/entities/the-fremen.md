# The Fremen

Indigenous people of Arrakis; ~5 million strong, living in desert sietches; expert survivors whom the Harkonnen underestimated and the Atreides are trying to befriend.

## Overview

The Fremen are the original inhabitants of [[arrakis]]. The official Imperial population counts dramatically understate their numbers — the Harkonnen killed them so casually that the real count was suppressed. [[liet-kynes]] estimates ~5 million live in the desert in hidden sietches.

They are the most advanced desert-survival society in the known universe. Their [[stillsuit]] technology is decades ahead of anything off-world. Their fighters are among the deadliest humans alive — generations of harsh selection.

## Culture

**Water as sacred resource.** Arrakis has no open water. All water is precious. The Fremen measure wealth in water. They recycle the water from their dead (the body's moisture returned to the tribe). Stilgar's greeting — spitting on the table — is an act of profound respect: offering your own body's water to a stranger.

**Blue-in-blue eyes.** The Fremen show no white in their eyes — the sclera and iris both tinted blue from lifetime [[melange]] consumption. This is how you recognize someone who has lived on Arrakis long enough.

**Sietches.** Desert communities, hidden in the rocky terrain. Location of sietches is secret. Size varies but each is a self-sufficient unit.

## The Prophecy

The [[bene-gesserit]] planted religious prophecies on Arrakis centuries ago through the [[missionaria-protectiva]]. These prophecies describe a messiah arriving from off-world, led by a woman who is Bene Gesserit. [[lady-jessica]] recognized these as planted myths — but the Fremen believe them genuinely.

This means [[paul-atreides]] and Jessica might be able to invoke these prophecies for protection and influence. A tool and a danger simultaneously.

## Potential Alliance

Under the Harkonnen, the Fremen were prey. The Atreides offer something different: respect and potential partnership. [[duke-leto-atreides]] is already meeting with Fremen leaders in Ch3. [[stilgar]]'s evaluation of Paul at the reception may be the beginning of something.

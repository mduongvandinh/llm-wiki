# Paul Atreides

Protagonist of Dune; 15-year-old heir to House Atreides, trained in Bene Gesserit techniques and possibly the Kwisatz Haderach.

## Profile

- **Full name:** Paul Atreides
- **Age:** 15
- **Faction:** [[house-atreides]]
- **Role:** Heir and protagonist
- **Status:** Alive (through Ch. 3)

## Personality & Motivations

Paul is unusually calm under pressure — demonstrated during the Gom Jabbar test when he controlled fear through mental discipline rather than instinct. He is perceptive beyond his years, able to read emotional states in others (a Bene Gesserit trait his mother trained into him). He is also deeply observant: on arrival at Arrakis he notices everything — the stillsuits, the Fremen's blue eyes, the cinnamon smell of spice in the air.

He is pulled between two worlds: his father's political pragmatism and his mother's spiritual/physical training. He shows respect to both, synthesizing them naturally.

## Key Relationships

- [[lady-jessica]]: His mother. She secretly trained him in Bene Gesserit techniques, defying the sisterhood's orders. Paul senses her anxiety during the Gom Jabbar test and is moved by it.
- [[duke-leto-atreides]]: His father. Paul respects and admires him deeply. Leto's quote about "the sleeper must awaken" clearly resonates with Paul.
- [[reverend-mother-mohiam]]: Tested him in Ch1. Paul surprised her with his calm and perception — he read *her* fear during the test. She found him "dangerous" and possibly the Kwisatz Haderach, but "one generation early."
- [[gurney-halleck]]: Weapons trainer. Treats Paul with warm roughness, like an older brother.
- [[stilgar]]: Met in Ch3. Stilgar evaluated Paul carefully — significance unclear but noted.

## Key Moments

- **Chapter 1:** Endured the Gom Jabbar test — placed hand in box causing intense pain while a poisoned needle was held to his neck. Survived through pure mental discipline.
- **Chapter 1:** Perceived the Reverend Mother's fear, demonstrating Bene Gesserit-level awareness despite being male.
- **Chapter 2:** Learned from Thufir Hawat that the move to Arrakis is almost certainly a political trap.
- **Chapter 3:** First sight of Arrakis. His prescient dreams become more vivid — the Fremen girl feels closer.

## Prescient Dreams

Since before Ch1, Paul has recurring dreams set on Arrakis — a desert landscape and a Fremen girl (unnamed). These dreams intensify on Arrakis in Ch3. The Reverend Mother called him "dangerous" partly because of this precognitive potential. [[kwisatz-haderach]] lore suggests the chosen one would be able to see across time.

## Notable Quotes

> "I can feel your fear. You're touching death." — Paul to the Reverend Mother, Ch1

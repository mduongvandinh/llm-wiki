# Notion — The All-in-One Workspace

## Company
- **Founded:** 2016
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Stage:** Late Stage / Pre-IPO (~$275M raised, valued at $10B in 2021)
- **Size:** ~500-800 employees
- **Target market:** Everyone — individuals, teams, enterprises

## Positioning
"One tool for your whole team. Write. Plan. Organize. Work together."

Core message: **infinite flexibility** — Notion is a blank canvas that can be anything. Wiki, project tracker, database, doc editor — all in one.

## Pricing (as of Q1 2026)
| Tier | Price | Key Features | Limits |
|------|-------|-------------|--------|
| Free | $0 | Unlimited pages, 10 guest collaborators | Limited block history |
| Plus | $10/user/mo | Unlimited history, unlimited guests | — |
| Business | $15/user/mo | Advanced permissions, analytics | — |
| Enterprise | Custom | Advanced security, SCIM, audit logs | — |
| Notion AI | +$8/user/mo | AI writing, summarization, Q&A | Add-on to any plan |

## Core Features
- **Docs:** Rich text editor with block-based content
- **Databases:** Tables, boards, calendars, galleries, lists, timelines
- **Wiki:** Nested pages, internal linking, team wiki
- **Projects:** Kanban/timeline view via database
- **Templates:** Thousands of community templates
- **Notion AI:** Integrated AI writing, summarization, Q&A on workspace content
- **API:** REST API for integrations
- **Web Clipper:** Browser extension to clip content

## What Makes Them Different
1. **Flexibility:** Can be configured for virtually any workflow
2. **All-in-one:** Replaces Confluence + Jira + Google Docs for many teams
3. **Community:** Massive template marketplace and creator community
4. **Consumer appeal:** One of few B2B tools with strong consumer adoption

## Known Weaknesses (from G2, Reddit, customer feedback)
- **Performance:** Slow on large databases and pages; "Notion lag" is a meme
- **Offline mode:** Poor offline experience; essentially requires internet
- **Too flexible = overwhelming:** New users often lost without a template to start
- **Not great for engineers:** No Git integration; issues lack sprint/velocity features
- **Export:** Difficult to export data in clean formats; lock-in concern
- **Permissions:** Complex permission model confuses teams; enterprise RBAC is limited on lower tiers
- **Real-time collaboration:** Lags behind Google Docs for simultaneous editing

## Recent Moves
- 2025-Q4: Notion AI Q&A — ask questions about your entire workspace
- 2025-Q3: Notion Sites — publish Notion pages as public websites
- 2025-Q2: Notion Calendar — standalone calendar app integrating with workspace
- 2025-Q1: Forms — native form builder replacing Typeform for simple use cases
- 2024: Raised additional funding; expanding enterprise sales team significantly

## Job Postings Signal (Q1 2026)
Heavily hiring:
- Enterprise Sales reps (multiple regions)
- Customer Success Managers (enterprise)
- ML Engineers (AI features)

**Signal:** Big push into Enterprise + continued AI investment.

## Market Perception
- Best known brand in "productivity tools" category
- Strong love/hate relationship: evangelists vs people who hate the flexibility
- Common use: Teams start free, expand across organization, then face enterprise pricing surprise
- AI add-on pricing ($8/user/mo extra) is controversial; users expected it to be included

## How to Beat Notion
- Win on performance / speed (linear already does this for eng teams)
- Win on structure / opinionated workflows (Notion is "too flexible")
- Win on offline / local-first
- Win on pricing transparency (no surprise AI add-on fees)
- Win on specific verticals (Notion is too generic — vertical-specific tools often win)

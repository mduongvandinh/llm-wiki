# Linear — Project Management for Software Teams

## Company
- **Founded:** 2019
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Stage:** Series B (~$35M raised as of 2022)
- **Size:** ~50-100 employees
- **Target market:** Software engineering teams (startups to mid-size)

## Positioning
"Linear is a purpose-built tool for planning and building products. It's fast, focused, and beautifully designed."

Core message: **speed + opinionated workflow** for engineers. They reject the "feature bloat" of Jira and the "infinite flexibility" of Notion. Every opinion is intentional.

## Pricing (as of Q1 2026)
| Tier | Price | Key Features | Limits |
|------|-------|-------------|--------|
| Free | $0 | Unlimited members, 250 issues | No integrations, no roadmaps |
| Standard | $8/user/mo | Unlimited issues, integrations, roadmaps | — |
| Plus | $14/user/mo | Admin controls, private teams, SLA | — |
| Enterprise | Custom | SSO, audit logs, dedicated support | — |

**Key pricing insight:** No per-seat limits on Free tier (unusual). Converts teams by hitting the 250-issue limit organically.

## Core Features
- **Issues:** Fast keyboard-driven issue creation (<100ms UI response is their benchmark)
- **Cycles:** Time-boxed sprints with built-in velocity tracking
- **Roadmaps:** Timeline view for initiative planning
- **Projects:** Group issues into projects with progress tracking
- **Triage:** Inbox-style view for managing incoming requests
- **Git integration:** Auto-close issues from commits; branch names from issues
- **Slack integration:** Create issues from Slack messages
- **API:** Full REST + GraphQL API

## What Makes Them Different
1. **Speed obsession:** Built on local-first architecture — UI responses in <50ms
2. **Opinionated defaults:** Less customizable than Jira/Asana, but works better out of the box for eng teams
3. **Design quality:** Best-looking PM tool in the market by most accounts
4. **Developer love:** Keyboard shortcuts, Git integration, API-first

## Known Weaknesses (from G2, Reddit, customer feedback)
- **Not great for non-engineering teams:** Marketing, design, ops teams find it limiting
- **Limited reporting:** Analytics and reporting are basic vs Jira or Asana
- **No time tracking:** Built-in time tracking missing; need integrations
- **Enterprise features lagging:** SSO/SAML only on Enterprise; RBAC limited
- **No resource management:** Can't track team capacity or workload balancing
- **Mobile app:** iOS/Android apps exist but significantly behind desktop

## Recent Moves
- 2025-Q4: Launched "Linear Asks" — async Q&A feature for teams
- 2025-Q3: Introduced AI features for issue summarization and auto-triage
- 2025-Q2: Raised additional funding (unconfirmed amount)
- 2025-Q1: Added "Initiatives" — higher-level planning above Projects

**Signal:** Enterprise AE + Solutions Engineer = pushing harder into Enterprise segment. Performance engineering = continuing speed focus.

## Market Perception
- r/projectmanagement: Universally praised by eng-focused teams
- Common switch pattern: Jira → Linear (simplicity), Notion → Linear (speed)
- Less common reverse: Some enterprise teams revert to Jira for compliance/reporting

## How to Beat Linear
- Target teams that need cross-functional collaboration (Linear is too eng-centric)
- Win on reporting / analytics
- Win on resource management / capacity planning
- Win on enterprise governance features

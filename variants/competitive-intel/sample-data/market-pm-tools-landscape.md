# Project Management Tools — Market Landscape 2026

## Market Size
- Global PM software market: ~$7B (2025), growing ~10% YoY
- Driven by: remote work normalization, AI feature arms race, enterprise digitalization
- Key segments: SMB ($0-$50/user/mo), Mid-Market ($50-$200/seat/mo), Enterprise (custom)

## Key Trends (2026)

### AI Integration
- All major players adding AI: issue summarization, auto-prioritization, smart search
- Linear: AI triage + issue summaries
- Notion: AI Q&A on workspace content
- Asana: AI project risk detection
- Jira: Atlassian Intelligence for suggestions

**Watch:** AI is becoming table stakes; differentiation shifts to quality of AI features.

### Vertical Specialization
- Generic tools losing to purpose-built: construction PM (Procore), creative PM (Loom+Notion), engineering PM (Linear)
- Opportunity: deeply vertical tools command premium pricing and stronger retention

### Enterprise Push
- Every tool is pushing upmarket: Linear Enterprise, Notion Enterprise expansion
- SMB market commoditizing; margins compressing
- Enterprise requires: SSO, SAML, audit logs, data residency, dedicated CSM

### Local-First / Performance
- "Notion is slow" complaints driving users to alternatives
- Linear built its brand on <50ms performance
- New entrants (Capacities, Craft) emphasizing performance

## Switching Patterns (from Reddit research)

**Most common switches:**
1. Jira → Linear (engineers tired of complexity)
2. Notion → Linear (engineers need real PM tool, not docs)
3. Asana → Monday (visual preference)
4. Excel/Sheets → Any tool (digital transformation)
5. ClickUp → [any simpler tool] (feature overwhelm)

**Rarely switch away from:**
- Jira (enterprise lock-in, compliance)
- Notion (deep customization investment)

## Pricing Trends
- Free tiers getting more generous (growth motion)
- AI add-ons controversial (Notion $8/user/mo extra)
- Annual billing discounts increasing (20-30%)
- Enterprise pricing opaque and negotiable

## Consolidation Risk
- Atlassian (Jira) could acquire any mid-market player
- Salesforce, ServiceNow, Microsoft positioning in enterprise
- VC-backed players: some will not survive to profitability

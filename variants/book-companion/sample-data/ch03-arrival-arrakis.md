# Dune — Chapter 3: Arrival on Arrakis

## Summary
House Atreides arrives on Arrakis. Paul and Jessica land and take a groundcar to the city of Arrakeen. They observe the desert for the first time. Paul meets Stilgar (Fremen leader) and Dr. Liet-Kynes (Imperial Planetologist) at a formal reception. The scale and harshness of Arrakis becomes real.

## Characters Introduced

**Stilgar**
- Naib (leader) of a Fremen sietch (community)
- Tall, lean, blue-in-blue eyes (all-blue from spice saturation — no white in eyes)
- Evaluates Paul and Jessica carefully, says little
- Clearly a man of great authority among the Fremen
- Spits on the table as greeting/respect (water ritual — explained by Kynes)

**Dr. Liet-Kynes**
- Imperial Planetologist — the Emperor's man studying Arrakis ecology
- Half-Fremen (his father was Pardot Kynes, the first Planetologist)
- Deeply connected to the Fremen; secretly their judge and prophet
- Has a secret dream: transforming Arrakis into a green world (centuries-long ecological project)
- Evaluates Paul very carefully

**Gurney Halleck** (more developed)
- Present at arrival; wary, scanning for threats
- First to notice something feels wrong (Harkonnen traps?)

## Key Observations: Arrakis

**The Desert (Bled)**
- Vast, overwhelming, alien
- Nothing like Caladan — no sea smell, no moisture
- The horizon seems too close/far — optical effects of dust
- Heat even at dawn; you can feel it through the ship hull

**Arrakeen (City)**
- The main city, previously Harkonnen capital
- Buildings designed to minimize moisture loss
- Windtraps everywhere — collect atmospheric moisture (precious)
- People wear stillsuits (full-body suits that recycle body moisture)

**Stillsuits**
- Essential survival gear on Arrakis
- Recycles 99.7% of body's moisture
- Fremen-made suits are far superior to off-world designs
- How well you wear a stillsuit = how serious you are about Arrakis

## Key Concepts

**Spice (Melange) — More Details**
- The smell of Arrakis: cinnamon-like odor everywhere = trace spice in atmosphere
- Fremen have blue-in-blue eyes from constant spice consumption
- Withdrawal from spice after addiction = death
- Melange appears in the desert, associated with sandworm activity

**Sandworms (Shai-Hulud)**
- Enormous creatures living beneath the sand
- Move in response to rhythmic vibrations (footsteps, machinery)
- Fremen revere them as sacred
- Connection to spice: the spice cycle involves sandworms (exact mechanism not yet clear)

**Water as Sacred Resource**
- Arrakis has no open water; all water is precious
- Fremen measure wealth in water
- Stilgar's spit = offering water = profound respect
- The dead are processed for their water (body's moisture returned to the tribe)

**Fremen**
- Indigenous people of Arrakis
- ~5 million living in desert sietches (communities)
- Harkonnen killed them casually; official population counts are lies
- Expert desert survivors; their stillsuits are decades ahead of technology
- Have a prophecy about a messiah coming from off-world (Bene Gesserit planted this)

## The Bene Gesserit Missionaria Protectiva
- Bene Gesserit have seeded religious prophecies on primitive worlds for centuries
- Purpose: if a Bene Gesserit is ever stranded, she can use these prophecies for protection
- Arrakis has such prophecies: a man from off-world will lead the Fremen, his mother will be a Bene Gesserit
- Jessica recognizes these as Missionaria Protectiva — and realizes she and Paul fit the prophecy perfectly
- This is both a tool and a danger
